# Doctor's Appointment Checklist: Lung Health for PLWH

Use this checklist during your medical visits to ensure you discuss important aspects of lung health and smoking cessation.

## Before Your Appointment
- [ ] Write down any symptoms you've noticed (cough, shortness of breath, chest pain, fatigue)
- [ ] Note how many cigarettes/other tobacco products you smoke per day
- [ ] List any previous quit attempts and what helped/didn't help
- [ ] Bring a list of all medications, including antiretroviral therapy (ART)

## During Your Appointment
### 1. Smoking Status & Cessation
- [ ] Tell your doctor you smoke and want to quit
- [ ] Ask about evidence‑based cessation options:
  - Nicotine replacement therapy (patch, gum, lozenge)
  - Prescription medications (bupropion, varenicline)
  - Counseling or support groups
- [ ] Discuss potential interactions between smoking‑cessation medications and your ART
- [ ] Set a quit date and ask for a follow‑up plan

### 2. Lung Cancer Risk Assessment
- [ ] Ask about your personal lung cancer risk based on smoking history and HIV status
- [ ] Discuss whether you meet criteria for low‑dose CT (LDCT) screening
- [ ] Inquire about signs/symptoms of lung cancer to watch for

### 3. General Lung Health
- [ ] Review your vaccination status (pneumococcal, flu, COVID‑19)
- [ ] Discuss any respiratory infections you've had recently
- [ ] Ask about pulmonary function tests if you have breathing concerns
- [ ] Talk about lifestyle changes to improve lung health (exercise, air quality)

### 4. Follow‑up & Resources
- [ ] Schedule a follow‑up appointment to monitor progress
- [ ] Ask for reliable patient education materials (brochures, websites)
- [ ] Request referrals to smoking‑cessation programs or specialists if needed
- [ ] Confirm how to contact your care team with questions between visits

## After Your Appointment
- [ ] Fill any prescriptions for smoking‑cessation aids
- [ ] Join a support group or download a cessation app
- [ ] Mark your quit date on your calendar
- [ ] Share your plan with a friend or family member for accountability

---

*This checklist is a tool to help you have a productive conversation with your healthcare provider. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice.*
